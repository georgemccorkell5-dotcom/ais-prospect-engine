## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Industries (Priority Order)
- **Banking / Community Banks** — Small IT teams, compliance pressure, need automation
- **Manufacturing** — Core AIS territory, need automation and controls
- **Food & Beverage** — Process control, compliance, quality automation
- **Healthcare / Senior Living** — Building automation, compliance, operational efficiency
- **Distribution / Logistics** — Warehouse automation, conveyor systems
- **Construction / Real Estate** — Building systems, smart building controls

### Company Profile
- **Size:** 20–500 employees (sweet spot for needing automation but not having in-house engineering)
- **Revenue:** $10M–$500M
- **Location:** Twin Cities metro, Minnesota, upper Midwest
- **Pain signals:** Small/no IT department, manual processes, aging infrastructure, compliance pressure, growth without corresponding tech investment

### Key Buyer Titles (Decision Makers)
- CEO / President / Owner (common in this size range)
- CFO / Finance Director
- VP of Operations
- IT Director / IT Leader
- Facilities Director

### Pain Signals to Look For
- No dedicated IT department or very small IT team
- Family-owned business (relationship-driven, loyal once trust is built)
- Multiple locations (complexity without scale)
- Manual or aging industrial processes
- Compliance/regulatory pressure (banking, healthcare, food)
- Recent growth or expansion
- Job postings for IT or operations roles (signal they need help)

### Disqualifiers
- Already has large in-house engineering/automation team
- National corporation with centralized IT purchasing
- No physical operations or industrial component
- Sub-$5M revenue (too small for meaningful engagement)

## MESSAGING

### Opening Hook Options
- "Most companies your size in the Twin Cities are running critical operations on systems that were installed 10+ years ago. We help modernize without disrupting what's working."
- "With [X] locations and a lean team, you're probably spending more time managing systems than growing the business. That's exactly the kind of problem we solve."

### Value Prop by Persona

**CEO / President / Owner:**
You built this business on doing things right. But as you've grown, the systems haven't kept up — and your team is spending more time on workarounds than on what actually moves the business forward. AIS modernizes your automation and controls so your people can focus on what they're good at, not fighting aging infrastructure.

**CFO / Finance Director:**
Every manual process is a hidden cost — in labor, in errors, in compliance risk. AIS helps companies your size automate the right things, with clear ROI timelines and no bloated enterprise pricing. We're local, we're accountable, and we don't disappear after install.

**IT Leader:**
You're probably wearing five hats and managing systems that weren't designed to talk to each other. AIS gives you a partner who handles the industrial automation side — PLC programming, SCADA, controls integration — so you can focus on the business technology stack.

### Proof Points
- Based in Minnesota — local presence, local accountability
- Full-service: engineering, fabrication, installation, and ongoing support
- Decades of experience in industrial automation across the upper Midwest
- Serve manufacturing, food & beverage, healthcare, and commercial sectors

### What NOT to Say
- Don't lead with technical jargon (PLC, SCADA, HMI) — lead with business outcomes
- Don't bash their current systems — respect what got them here
- Don't over-scope — start with one pain point, prove value, then expand
- Don't position as a vendor — position as a long-term partner
- Don't assume they know what industrial automation means for their business

## COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### Direct Competitors
- Local electrical contractors doing basic automation
- National integrators (Rockwell, Siemens partners) — often too expensive/slow for this segment
- IT MSPs trying to expand into OT/industrial space

### Indirect Competitors
- Internal "we'll figure it out" approach
- Hiring a single automation engineer (expensive, single point of failure)
- Doing nothing and living with manual processes

### Our Angle
- Local Minnesota company — not a faceless national vendor
- Full lifecycle support — not just install and walk away
- Right-sized for mid-market — enterprise capability without enterprise pricing
- Deep industrial domain expertise (not just IT people dabbling in automation)

## QUALIFYING CRITERIA

### Must-Haves
- [ ] Has physical operations, manufacturing, or facilities requiring controls/automation
- [ ] Located in Minnesota / upper Midwest (AIS service area)
- [ ] 20+ employees (enough complexity to need help)
- [ ] Budget authority for capital or service investments

### Strong Signals (Prioritize These Leads)
- No dedicated IT department or understaffed IT
- Family-owned or privately held (relationship buyers)
- Multiple locations in the metro
- Aging infrastructure or manual processes visible from outside
- Recent expansion, new facility, or acquisition
- Compliance pressure (banking regulators, FDA, OSHA)

### Deal Breakers
- Has large in-house automation engineering team
- National HQ outside Minnesota with centralized purchasing
- No physical/industrial operations
- Government entity with rigid procurement
