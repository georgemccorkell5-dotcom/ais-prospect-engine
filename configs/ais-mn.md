## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Top Verticals (30–500 employees)

**Manufacturing & Industrial**
- Why: Heavy MN presence, typically 100–400 employees, weak cybersecurity, increasing ransomware targeting
- Pain points: OT + IT network segmentation, compliance (CMMC, NIST, ISO), downtime = money (high urgency)
- Positioning: Proactive IT & cybersecurity management + uptime + compliance = efficient and safe business

**Healthcare**
- Who: Specialty clinics, med device startups, healthcare services (30–400 employees)
- Pain points: HIPAA + PHI protection, 24/7 uptime requirements, cybersecurity gaps
- Positioning: Managed infrastructure and cybersecurity to remain compliant and reduce risk of downtime

**Financial Services**
- Who: Wealth management, credit unions, banks, insurance agencies
- Regulatory drivers: SEC (for RIAs), FINRA, GLBA, cyber insurance mandates
- Pain points: Audit pressure (constant), vendor risk management, identity + access control gaps, legacy systems
- What they buy: SIEM, SOC, MDR, vulnerability management, pentesting
- Positioning: Designated financial helpdesk team to pass audits, reduce risk, and prove compliance continuously

**Education**
- Who: Large districts, private schools, small colleges/universities
- Why: Lean IT teams, little cybersecurity expertise, big ransomware targets, sensitive data (student records, parent banking info, PII), distributed users (staff + students)
- Positioning: Designated education helpdesk team providing IT management and cybersecurity protection to proactively protect students and reduce risk of downtime

**Legal**
- Who: Firms between 50–250 employees, small internal IT teams
- Pain points: Email compromise (biggest security issue), poor security controls, lack of monitoring/incident response, IT leaders filling out long vendor risk questionnaires
- What they need: MDR/SOC/24x7 monitoring, DLP, vCISO (policy guidance)
- Positioning: Dedicated legal helpdesk team to protect clients, fill out questionnaires, prevent reputational damage

### Company Profile
- **Size:** 30–500 employees (sweet spot for needing managed IT/security but not having sufficient in-house capability)
- **Revenue:** $10M–$500M
- **Location:** Twin Cities metro, Minnesota
- **Pain signals:** Small/no IT department, reactive IT support, compliance pressure, cybersecurity gaps, audit failures, insurance requirements tightening, legacy systems

### Key Buyer Titles (Decision Makers)
- CEO / President / Owner
- CFO / Finance Director
- IT Leader (CIO, CISO, IT Director, IT Manager)

### Pain Signals to Look For
- No dedicated IT department or very small IT team
- Family-owned business (relationship-driven, loyal once trust is built)
- Multiple locations (complexity without scale)
- Reactive IT support — only hear from provider when something breaks
- Compliance/regulatory pressure (HIPAA, CMMC, SEC, FINRA, GLBA)
- Cyber insurance requirements tightening
- Recent growth or expansion
- Job postings for IT, security, or operations roles (signal they need help)
- Audit failures or remediation in progress
- Email compromise incidents or ransomware concerns

### Disqualifiers
- Already has large in-house IT/security team (50+ IT staff)
- National corporation with centralized IT purchasing
- Sub-$5M revenue (too small for meaningful engagement)
- Already locked into a long-term MSP/MSSP contract (unless unhappy)

## MESSAGING

### Opening Hook Options
- "How confident are you in your current IT and cybersecurity coverage?"
- "We've been working with organizations in the Twin Cities that thought they were in good shape, but uncovered gaps around monitoring, response, and compliance once we took a closer look."
- "As insurance and compliance requirements have gotten stricter, a lot of teams are finding gaps they didn't know they had."

### Value Prop by Persona

**CEO / President / Owner:**
You built this business on doing things right. But between compliance requirements tightening, cyber insurance getting more demanding, and threats getting more sophisticated, the IT side has gotten complex fast. AIS gives you a proactive technology partner — not just break-fix support — so you can focus on running the business with confidence that your systems and data are protected.

**CFO / Finance Director:**
Every audit finding, every compliance gap, every hour of downtime is a cost — in real dollars and in risk. AIS helps companies your size get proactive IT management and real cybersecurity coverage, with clear accountability and no bloated enterprise pricing. We're local, we're backed nationally on the security side, and we prove our value continuously — not just at renewal.

**IT Leader (CIO, CISO, IT Director, IT Manager):**
You're probably wearing five hats — managing infrastructure, fielding tickets, handling security, prepping for audits, and filling out vendor risk questionnaires. AIS gives you a partner who takes the security and monitoring load off your plate with SOC, MDR, and 24/7 coverage, so you can focus on the strategic work that actually moves the business forward.

### Proof Points
- Based in the Twin Cities — local presence, local accountability
- Nationally backed MSSP — enterprise-grade security for mid-market budgets
- Dedicated vertical helpdesk teams (financial, education, legal, healthcare)
- Proactive monitoring, regular testing, clear communication — not just break-fix
- Help clients pass audits, meet compliance, and satisfy cyber insurance requirements

### What NOT to Say
- Don't lead with acronyms (SIEM, SOC, MDR) — lead with business outcomes
- Don't bash their current IT provider — acknowledge what they've built
- Don't over-scope — start with one pain point, prove value, then expand
- Don't position as a vendor — position as a long-term partner
- Don't say "just reaching out" or "hope this finds you well"
- Don't be salesy or desperate — peer-to-peer, consultative tone
