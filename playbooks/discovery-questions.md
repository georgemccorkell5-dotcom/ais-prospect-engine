# Discovery Questions Playbook

## PURPOSE
These frameworks help qualify prospects and guide discovery calls. Pull from these when prepping for meetings or when a cold email gets a reply and moves to a call.

---

## STAGE 1: SITUATION (Understand Their World)

- Walk me through what happens after one of your reps finishes a sales call today.
- How are your reps currently logging call notes and deal updates?
- What CRM are you on, and how would you rate your team's adoption?
- How many reps are on the team? What does the sales motion look like?
- What does your sales cycle typically look like — number of touches, length?

## STAGE 2: PAIN (Find the Hurt)

- When you look at your pipeline right now, how confident are you in the data?
- How much time do your managers spend chasing reps for call updates?
- Have you ever lost a deal or been surprised at a forecast review because the CRM was missing context?
- What happens when a rep leaves — how much deal knowledge walks out the door?
- If I looked at your CRM data for any given deal, would I know what happened on the last call?

## STAGE 3: IMPACT (Quantify the Cost)

- What does a missed follow-up or lost deal context cost you in revenue?
- How much time per week do you estimate your reps spend on CRM data entry?
- If your pipeline data was 90% accurate instead of 40%, what would that mean for your forecast?
- How does incomplete CRM data affect your ability to coach reps?

## STAGE 4: SOLUTION FIT (Bridge to Our Product)

- If your reps could just do a brain dump after every call and have it organized automatically — would they actually do it?
- What would it mean for your team if every call debrief was structured, complete, and in your CRM within minutes?
- What would need to be true for you to pilot something like this?
- Who else on your team would need to be involved in evaluating a tool like this?

## STAGE 5: NEXT STEPS (Advance the Deal)

- Based on what we discussed, I think there is a fit. What would a pilot look like on your end?
- Who else should be in the room for a deeper demo?
- What is your timeline for addressing this — this quarter or further out?
- What is the typical process for bringing in a new sales tool?

---

## QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST (Use After Discovery)

After a discovery call, confirm before marking as qualified:

- [ ] They use a major CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Dynamics, etc.) — or have enough sales process to benefit from structured notes
- [ ] They have 5+ reps doing multi-touch, consultative sales
- [ ] CRM data quality is a recognized pain point
- [ ] Clear buyer identified (title and authority confirmed)
- [ ] Timeline exists (not "someday")
- [ ] Budget exists or can be created for sales tools
- [ ] Open to AI-powered solutions
