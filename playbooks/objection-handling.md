# Objection Handling Playbook

## FRAMEWORK
For every objection: ACKNOWLEDGE then REFRAME then EVIDENCE then REDIRECT

Never argue. Never get defensive. Objections are buying signals.

---

## We already use Gong / Chorus

Acknowledge: Great tools for call recording and analysis.

Reframe: Gong tells you what happened AFTER the call. We prepare reps BEFORE with the right questions, then structure their debrief AFTER, catching gaps and feeding clean data into Salesforce. We sit alongside Gong, not instead of it. And we work with whatever CRM you're on.

Redirect: Would it be useful to see how the two work together?

---

## Our reps will not adopt another tool

Acknowledge: Adoption is the number one killer of sales tech.

Reframe: That is exactly why we built this around the brain dump. Reps just talk or type what happened and we handle the rest. Less work than what they do now, not more.

Redirect: What if we piloted with 2-3 of your most resistant reps?

---

## We are not ready for AI tools yet

Acknowledge: There is a lot of noise in the AI space right now.

Reframe: This is not AI for AI sake. It solves a specific problem: getting what reps know into your CRM without data entry. AI is just the engine. The outcome is better pipeline data.

Redirect: What would need to be true for you to feel ready?

---

## How is this different from ChatGPT

Acknowledge: A lot of teams are experimenting with that.

Reframe: ChatGPT does not know your CRM data, your sales process, or what questions reps should have asked. We are purpose-built for the sales workflow, integrated with your CRM, and designed to improve with your specific process.

Redirect: Want to see a side-by-side comparison?

---

## We do not have budget right now

Acknowledge: Budgets are tight everywhere.

Reframe: Quick math. If each rep spends 30 min/day on CRM data entry, that is 2.5 hours/week of lost selling time. Multiply by team size and hourly revenue contribution. The cost of NOT fixing this is usually 10-50x the per-seat fee.

Redirect: Would a quick ROI estimate based on your team size help?

---

## We need to think about it

Acknowledge: Of course, worth thinking through.

Reframe: To help you think it through, what are the main questions or concerns?

Redirect: Would a one-pager for your team help? Can we put 15 min on the calendar next week?

---

## Send me some information

This is often a polite brush-off. Test for real interest.

Response: Happy to. What specifically would be most useful? Technical integration details, the ROI case, or how the rep workflow actually works?

If they get specific, that signals real interest. Send targeted info and book a follow-up.
If they stay vague, likely a brush-off. Send a concise one-pager and plan a follow-up sequence.
