# Cold Outreach Playbook

## GOLDEN RULES
1. Every cold email must be under 120 words
2. Opening line is ALWAYS about the prospect, never about us
3. One pain point, one value prop, one CTA per email
4. Subject lines: 3-6 words, curiosity-driven, lowercase is fine
5. CTA must be low-friction ("worth a conversation?" not "book a 60-min demo")
6. NEVER use em dashes (—) anywhere in the email. Use commas, periods, or rewrite the sentence instead.

## EMAIL FRAMEWORK: THE PAIN-BRIDGE

**Subject:** [specific reference to their world]

**Body:**

[HOOK — 1 sentence about THEM. Reference something specific: a job posting, a LinkedIn post, a company initiative, recent news.]

[PAIN — 1-2 sentences connecting that to a pain point from our config. Make them feel seen.]

[BRIDGE — 1-2 sentences on how we solve it. Outcome-focused, not feature-focused.]

[PROOF — 1 sentence of credibility. Cambria beta, relevant stat, or social proof.]

[CTA — 1 sentence. Low friction, specific.]

### Example (AI Sales Closer):

**Subject:** your reps' post-call notes

Hi [Name],

Noticed [Company] is scaling the sales team — congrats on the growth. Curious: as you add reps, how are you making sure what they learn on calls actually makes it into your CRM?

We built a tool that lets reps do a raw brain dump after any meeting, then structures it into clean, CRM-ready data — and flags if they missed anything critical. Think of it as a debrief coach that also does their data entry.

We've been testing it with leadership at a $750M manufacturer. Would it be worth 15 minutes to see if it fits your world?

[Your name]
Monarch Technology Group

## EMAIL FRAMEWORK: THE QUESTION OPENER

**Subject:** quick question about [their process]

**Body:**

[QUESTION — Ask a genuine, thought-provoking question related to their pain]

[CONTEXT — Brief explanation of why you're asking / what you've seen]

[OFFER — What you can show them]

[CTA]

### Example:

**Subject:** quick question about CRM data

Hi [Name],

After your team's sales calls, what percentage of the conversation actually makes it into your CRM?

Most sales leaders I talk to say it's somewhere around 30-40% — and that gap is killing their forecasts. We've built something that captures the other 60% without adding any work for reps.

Worth a quick look?

## EMAIL FRAMEWORK: THE REFERRAL / WARM INTRO

For when you have a connection or mutual context.

**Subject:** [mutual connection] suggested I reach out

**Body:**

[CONNECTION — How you're connected. Be specific.]

[RELEVANCE — Why this matters to them specifically.]

[ASK — Clear, low-friction next step.]

---

## FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE (After No Response)

### Follow-Up 1 (Day 3)
- Short (2-3 sentences)
- Add a new angle or insight — don't just "bump"
- Example: Share a relevant stat, article, or observation about their industry

### Follow-Up 2 (Day 7)
- Different approach entirely
- Try a question-based email or share a relevant insight
- Reference the original email briefly

### Follow-Up 3 (Day 14)
- The "breakup" email
- Respectful, door-open tone
- Example: "Seems like the timing might not be right — totally get it. If CRM data quality ever becomes a priority, I'm easy to find."

### After 3 No-Responses
- Move to nurture list (revisit in 60-90 days)
- Or archive if clearly not a fit

## SUBJECT LINE BANK
- your reps' post-call notes
- quick question about CRM data
- what happens after the sales call
- CRM data quality
- [their company] + sales efficiency
- your sales team's blind spots
- saw you're hiring AEs
- brain dump → CRM (in a good way)

## WORDS TO AVOID IN COLD EMAIL
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "Just reaching out"
- "Touching base"
- "Innovative" / "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing"
- "Synergy"
- "Circle back"
- "Pick your brain"
- "No-brainer"
