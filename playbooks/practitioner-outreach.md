# Practitioner Recruitment Outreach Playbook

## CONTEXT
This is NOT a sales email. This is a **partnership recruitment** email. We're inviting elite marketing practitioners to license their methodology into AI agents. The tone is peer-to-peer, respectful of their expertise, and focused on what's in it for them.

## GOLDEN RULES
1. Use their **name** in the greeting, always "Hi [First Name]"
2. Opening line references something **specific** about their work (a framework, a book, a tool, a talk)
3. Keep it under 150 words, practitioners are busy people
4. Tone: respectful, direct, peer-to-peer. NOT salesy, NOT fawning
5. NEVER say "I'm a big fan", show you know their work by citing specifics
6. Frame as an opportunity, not a pitch. They have something valuable, we have a way to scale it
7. CTA: low friction, "worth a 15-minute call?" or "open to exploring this?"
8. NEVER use em dashes (—) anywhere in the email. Use commas, periods, or rewrite the sentence instead.

## EMAIL FRAMEWORK: THE METHODOLOGY HOOK

**Subject:** [their specific framework/methodology name]

**Body:**

Monarch Technology Group

## TIER-SPECIFIC GUIDELINES

### Tier 1 (Icons — Neil Patel, Gary Vee, Alex Hormozi, Scott Galloway)
- SHORT subject line, no fluff
- Show you understand their specific methodology, not just their fame
- Expect it goes through a team — make it compelling enough for a gatekeeper to forward
- Patient — may need multiple touches or warm intro
- Don't be starstruck — peer-to-peer tone

### Tier 2 (Authorities — Rand Fishkin, Amy Porterfield, Brian Dean, Ryan Deiss, etc.)
- Direct outreach via email or LinkedIn is viable
- Reference their specific framework/book/tool by name
- These people are accessible and often respond to well-crafted cold emails
- Be specific about how THEIR methodology maps to the platform

### Tier 3 (Niche Experts — Perry Marshall, Peep Laja, Jay Abraham, etc.)
- Most accessible, often most enthusiastic
- They may feel undervalued relative to their expertise — our pitch validates them
- Emphasize that we want THE expert, not a generic approach
- "You're the person the industry cites for [topic]" is a powerful hook

## FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

### Follow-Up 1 (Day 4)
- Share a specific insight about how their methodology maps to the platform
- Example: "I was re-reading [their book/framework] and the [specific section] maps perfectly to how we'd structure the [channel] agent."

### Follow-Up 2 (Day 10)
- Different angle — social proof or scarcity
- "We're building partnerships with 5 practitioners per channel — [channel] is filling up"
- Or share a relevant development: "Just launched [feature], and it made me think of your [framework]"

### Follow-Up 3 (Day 18)
- Graceful door-open
- "Seems like timing might not be right. If you ever want to explore having your methodology run at scale via AI, I'm easy to find."

## SUBJECT LINE BANK
- [their framework name] as an AI agent
- scaling [their name]'s methodology
- your [book/course] — running live
- [their framework] + AI execution
- quick question about licensing your system
- the next chapter for [their methodology]
- [their company] methodology × AI

## WORDS TO AVOID
- "Fan" / "big fan" / "love your work" (show, don't tell)
- "Influencer" (they're practitioners, not influencers)
- "Replace" / "automate you" (it's scaling them, not replacing them)
- "AI tool" (it's an execution agent, not a tool)
- "Passive income" (sounds like a scam)
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "Just reaching out"
- "Pick your brain"
